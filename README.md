# IBM_Exploration
This repository contains the initial exploration phase for developing an AI-powered virtual pet aimed at providing companionship and support for elderly users. The project leverages Unity for building an interactive environment and IBM Watson for natural language processing and voice interactions.


This project explores the development of an AI-powered virtual “social pet” designed to assist and engage elderly users. The virtual pet will provide companionship, encourage social interaction, and offer practical support through voice-based communication using IBM Watson services (Speech to Text, Text to Speech, and Watson Assistant). The system will be built in Unity, focusing on user-friendly design, emotional engagement, and real-time assistance.

Key features of the virtual pet include:
	•	Daily Routine Monitoring: Tracks the user’s routine and notifies caregivers if unusual activity is detected.
	•	Conversational Engagement: Prompts users to share their day and plan upcoming activities.
	•	Personalized Recommendations: Suggests music, podcasts, and other content based on user preferences.
	•	Messaging Assistance: Reads messages from family members and allows users to respond via voice.
	•	Emotional Interaction: Displays emotions (happy, sad, etc.) based on user interaction levels.

This project will initially focus on exploring tools, technologies, and frameworks necessary for development, including setting up Unity, integrating IBM Watson APIs, and designing basic virtual pet interactions.

Based on IBM’s design principles, our project will emphasize human-centered design, empathy-driven solutions, and accessibility. We will adopt IBM’s Enterprise Design Thinking approach to foster collaboration and innovation, ensuring our virtual “social pet” for the elderly is inclusive, intuitive, and impactful. The design will adhere to IBM’s standards of simplicity, clarity, and scalability, focusing on user needs and continuous improvement. This project will also reflect IBM’s commitment to diversity and social responsibility.
IBM Design Principles

The global population is aging at an unprecedented rate, leading to significant shifts in demographic structures and creating new challenges for societies worldwide. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the proportion of people aged 60 and above is expected to double from 12% to 22% between 2015 and 2050. This rapid growth is largely attributed to declining fertility rates and improving life expectancy. While longer lifespans are undoubtedly a testament to medical and technological advancements, they also place increasing pressure on healthcare systems, social services, and economic policies designed to support older adults.

One of the most pressing issues arising from an aging population is the risk of social isolation and loneliness. Many seniors live alone, have limited mobility, or face reduced opportunities for social interaction—factors that can negatively impact both mental and physical well-being. Consequently, maintaining independence, enhancing quality of life, and ensuring emotional support have become key focus areas in geriatric care.

In light of these challenges, companion robots offer a promising solution. These robots can provide consistent interaction, support daily activities, and even act as intermediaries for remote health monitoring and communication with caregivers or family members. By offering a form of social engagement and practical assistance, companion robots have the potential to alleviate feelings of loneliness while promoting greater autonomy among the elderly. As research and development in robotics continue to advance, the integration of artificial intelligence, natural language processing, and intuitive user interfaces stands to further improve the effectiveness and adoption of these technologies. Exploring these possibilities not only addresses current needs but also lays the groundwork for more robust systems and policies that can adapt to the evolving demands of an aging population.
